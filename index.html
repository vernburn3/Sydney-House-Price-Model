Sydney House Price Model

Methodology & Framework

1. The Problem with Medians

The Sydney property market is highly heterogeneous. Comparing a 400 m² unrenovated lot to a 700 m² premium build in the exact same suburb is mathematically flawed if you rely solely on raw median price tracking. Median prices are easily skewed by the composition of sales in any given quarter (e.g., if more mansions sell than cottages, the median spikes artificially).

This dashboard was built to establish a standardized, bivariate baseline. By decoupling land area and property "quality" into normalized Z-scores, we can isolate true localized market movements from the structural noise of individual asset variance.

2. Mathematical Basis

Real estate pricing and land geometry naturally exhibit a severe positive skew, necessitating a log-normal transformation for accurate statistical modeling. Let \( P \) represent the sale price and \( A \) represent the land area. We define \( L = \ln(A) \).

For any given SA2 region and year, the natural log of the area (\( L \)) is distributed with mean \( \mu_L \) and standard deviation \( \sigma_L \). A property's Area Z-score is therefore:

$$ Z_L = \frac{L - \mu_L}{\sigma_L} $$

The model assumes the final price is exponentially proportional to the log-area, scaled by a localized structural coefficient \( R \):

$$ P = e^{R \cdot L} \times 1,000,000 $$

By reversing this formula on historical sales data, we derive the structural coefficient \( R \) for every sale. The distribution of \( R \) within an SA2 region gives us \( \mu_R \) and \( \sigma_R \). A property's Quality Z-score (\( Z_R \)) quantifies how many standard deviations its finish, view, or architecture sits above or below the regional baseline:

$$ Z_R = \frac{R - \mu_R}{\sigma_R} $$

This framework allows us to project the fair value of a theoretical property at any point in time by recombining a target year's parameters with a property's fixed \( Z_L \) and estimated \( Z_R \).

3. Data Sourcing & Handling

Sourcing: Historical sales records and raw pricing data are aggregated from the NSW Valuer General. Geographic coordinate mapping utilizes the Geocoded National Address File (G-NAF), which is then overlaid onto official NSW Spatial Services cadastral boundaries to ensure precise lot rendering.

Handling: To maintain statistical integrity, the dataset is scrubbed of non-market transfers. High-density strata titles (apartments) and extreme geometric outliers (e.g., highly irregular zoning anomalies or split-blocks) are filtered prior to calculating regional parameters to prevent distribution skew.

4. Practical Application
Scenario: Evaluating a Premium Build in Hunters Hill

Imagine looking at a 500 m² block in Hunters Hill that recently underwent a high-end architectural renovation. Referencing the raw median sale price for Hunters Hill will not tell you if the asking price is mathematically fair for this specific caliber of home.

  1. We determine the Area Z-score (\( Z_L \)) by comparing \( \ln(500) \) to the historical baseline parameters for the Hunters Hill SA2 region.
  2. We assign a Quality Z-score (\( Z_R \)) of roughly \( +1.5 \) to reflect the premium architectural finishes (sitting in the top ~7% of regional stock).
  3. We feed these specific parameters into the Estimator Tab alongside the current year's \( \mu_R \) and \( \sigma_R \) values.

The resulting calculation provides an objective, baseline fair-value stripped of short-term market hysteria, allowing an analyst to accurately measure the premium being demanded.
